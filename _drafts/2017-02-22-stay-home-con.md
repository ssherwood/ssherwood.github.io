I recommend that developers go to at least one technical conference every other year (even more
frequently if possible).  The actual event itself doesn't matter as much as the opportunity to
escape the confines of the local office to socialize with developers outside your immediate
circles as well as to attend technical sessions that inspire new ideas or introduce new tools
or techniques to your intellectual tool chest.

However, some years, budgets are tight; other years, deadlines are looming.  Even when things seem
to be about to work out, life can happen.  For whatever reason, you may find yourself in the
situation of not being able to attend any conferences but still would like to get access to some
technical content to refresh and invigorate the mind.

# The Negotiation

First, negotiate with your boss to take a few days off to "work from home".  Make it clear to them
that you intend to spend the time attending virtual sessions on technical topics in lieu of a full
conference.  Point out that you will be saving the company money by not having to pay for travel,
food and expensive hotels.  Also make it clear that, like a conference, you will be responding less
frequently to emails or other queries due to being off-site.

It is also important to set expectations with your family that you are working and should not be
disturbed during your work day (set a clear timeframe that gives you more than a typical day).
Obviously, in case of dire emergencies you can be there to assist.
 
Make sure that they can agree to your terms.

# Planing Your Event

Find a time where you can
completely clear you calendar (and keep it clear to avoid potential work-related distractions).
Next, sit down and browse YouTube and/or the links provided and build your own "Con" itinerary.
Pick videos that will interest or challenge yourself -- don't be afraid to pick a few topics way
outside of your comfort zone.  Finally, put them on your calendar and let your boss know.
